SPECIAL FIELD ORDERS,
 HDQRS. MIL. DIV. OF THE MISS., Numbers 2.
 In the Field, Tunnel Hill, Ga., May 9, 1864.

The commanding general has just received the following telegram, and announces it with pleasure, viz:

WASHINGTON, D. C., May 9, 1864-11.15 a. m.

Major-General SHERMAN:

The Army of the Potomac had hard fighting on the 5th and 6th, driving the enemy from every position. On the 7th they had retreated some ten miles. General Grant in pursuit. They left their dead and wounded in our hands. Loss on both sides heavy. Battle-field near the Old Wilderness Tavern. Announce the victory to your army.

H. W. HALLECK,

Major-General and Chief of Staff.

Let us do likewise.

By order of Major General W. T. Sherman:

L. M. DAYTON,

Aide-de-Camp.

Mounted Infantry.
