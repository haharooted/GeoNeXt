HEADQUARTERS DISTRICT OF NORTH MISSOURI, Saint Joseph, Mo., April 14, 1864.

CLARK H. GREEN, HUGHES, and others, Glasgow, Mo.:

GENTLEMEN: I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your valued communication of 9th instant, and thank you for the information therein contained. I assure you that my entire energies shall be directed to the putting down of disorder and the restoration of tranquillity among the people of my district. There will be no letting up of military power until the safety of the State will permit it.

I hope soon to put more troops in your country, and trust by constant vigilance to weed out the marauders.

I am, gentleman, very respectfully, your obedient servant,

CLINTON B. FISK,

Brigadier-General.
