HEADQUARTERS DEPARTMENT OF THE GULF,

New Orleans, La., January 25, 1864

Brigadier General G. L. ANDREWS,

Commanding Port Hudson and Dependencies:

GENERAL: I am directed by the major-general commanding the department to communicate to you the following extract from a letter just received by him from Major General H. W. Halleck, General-in-Chief of the Army:

General Andrews' neglect of orders in regard to the river batteries at Port Hudson deserves a reprimand, if not a more serious punishment.

Very respectfully, I am, general, your most obedient servant,

CHAS. P. STONE.

Brigadier-General, Chief of Staff.
