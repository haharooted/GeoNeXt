FRANCIS BRYANT, DECEASED.  PURSUANT to the Act of Parliament 22nd and 23rd Vic., Cap. 35, entitled. " An Act to further amend the Law of property and to relieve trustees." MI CREDITORS and other persons having CLAIMS against the Estate of FRANCIS BRYANT, late of BLAIR HILL, Pauxisrous, near roots, in the County of Dorset, Esquire, deceased, ho died at Blake Hill aforesaid, on the 15th day of HAT, 1860, are hereby required to send in their Claims to the REVEREND FRANCIS JOHN BRYANT, of Notre BRENTON, Taturroci, in the County of Devon, and GEORGE LEDGARD, of Foots, aforesaid, Esquire, the Executors of the deceased, or to FREDERICK MAYHEW, their Solicitor, at his Chambers, No. 11. ARGYIN REGENT Smarr, in the County of Middlesex, on or before the 25th day of JULY next, at the expiration of which time the Executors will proceed to distribute the assets of the said FRANCIS BRYANT among the parties entitled thereto, having regard only to the Claims of which the Executors shall then have had notice and will not be liable for the assets so distributed to any person of whose claim they shall not have had notice at the time of such distribution.  Dated this 12th day of June, 1880. FREDERICK MAYHEW, 11, Argyle Place, Regent Street, Solicitor to the Executors.
